# Digital Minimalism

We carry supercomputers in our pockets and use them to argue with strangers. Something has gone wrong.

## The Attention Economy

Your attention is the most valuable resource you possess. It is finite, non-renewable within a given day, and under constant siege. Every app on your phone was designed by teams of engineers whose explicit goal is to capture and hold as much of your attention as possible.

This is not a conspiracy theory. It is a business model.

> "It is difficult to get a man to understand something when his salary depends upon his not understanding it."
> — Upton Sinclair

The infinite scroll, the pull-to-refresh, the notification badge — these are not features. They are **mechanisms of extraction**.

## The Cost

What do we lose when our attention is fragmented?

- **Deep work** — The ability to focus on cognitively demanding tasks for extended periods
- **Presence** — Being fully in a conversation, a meal, a moment
- **Boredom** — The fertile emptiness from which creativity emerges
- **Solitude** — Time alone with our own thoughts, unmediated by screens

Cal Newport argues that we have lost the ability to be alone with ourselves. We fill every gap — every elevator ride, every queue, every red light — with a screen. We have become afraid of our own minds.

## A Different Approach

Digital minimalism is not about rejecting technology. It is about being **intentional** with it.

The framework is simple:

1. **Audit** — List every digital tool you use regularly
2. **Question** — For each one, ask: "Does this directly support something I deeply value?"
3. **Remove** — Eliminate everything that doesn't pass the test
4. **Constrain** — For what remains, set boundaries on when and how you use it

This is harder than it sounds. The tools we use have become entangled with our identities. Leaving a platform feels like leaving a community. Turning off notifications feels like disconnecting from the world.

But the world existed before notifications. And you existed before your phone.

## The Quiet Life

There is a version of your life that is quieter. Not silent — quiet. A life where you check your phone because you *choose* to, not because a red badge commands it. Where your evenings belong to you. Where boredom returns, and with it, the strange and wonderful thoughts that only emerge in empty space.

```
// Before
while (awake) {
  checkPhone();
  scroll();
  react();
  repeat();
}

// After
while (awake) {
  think();
  create();
  rest();
  connect(intentionally);
}
```

## Begin

You don't have to do everything at once. Start with one thing:

Delete one app today. Just one. The one you reach for when you're not even sure why. The one that steals five minutes and returns nothing.

See how it feels.

The silence might surprise you.
