# The Weight of Words

Language is not neutral. Every word we choose carries history, connotation, and consequence. When we write, we are not merely transcribing thought — we are *constructing* it.

## Choosing is Closing

Consider the difference between these:

- "The door was **shut**."
- "The door was **closed**."
- "The door was **sealed**."

Each sentence describes the same physical state. But *shut* implies force. *Closed* is neutral, domestic. *Sealed* suggests permanence, even danger. The word you choose doesn't just describe reality — it **creates** it in the reader's mind.

## The Tyranny of Precision

There is a tension in writing between precision and resonance. A precise word hits one target exactly. A resonant word vibrates, suggesting multiple meanings at once.

> "The difference between the almost right word and the right word is really a large matter — it's the difference between the lightning bug and the lightning."
> — Mark Twain

Technical writing demands precision. Literature demands resonance. The best writing finds both.

## Words We've Lost

Languages are dying at an extraordinary rate. With each language lost, entire ways of seeing the world disappear:

| Word | Language | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| *Saudade* | Portuguese | A deep longing for something absent |
| *Komorebi* | Japanese | Sunlight filtering through leaves |
| *Waldeinsamkeit* | German | The feeling of solitude in a forest |
| *Toska* | Russian | A spiritual anguish without cause |

These are not just vocabulary items. They are **evidence** that human experience is richer than any single language can express.

## Writing as Thinking

The act of writing is not the act of recording pre-formed thoughts. It is the process by which thoughts *form*. You do not know what you think until you write it down.

This is why journaling works. This is why the essay exists as a form — from the French *essayer*, meaning "to try." An essay is an attempt. A reaching toward understanding through the act of arranging words.

## Silence

Sometimes the most powerful choice is no word at all.

The pause in a speech. The white space on a page. The sentence that ends before it should—

These silences carry weight precisely because they *refuse* to fill the space. They trust the reader to complete the thought.

---

*Write carefully. Every word is a brick in the house your reader will live in.*
